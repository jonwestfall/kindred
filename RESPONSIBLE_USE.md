## Responsible Use

Kindred is designed for reflection, creativity, and practice. It is not a therapist, crisis service, medical device, or substitute for professional mental health care.

Kindred should be used as a bounded self-reflection tool: a way to think through feelings, rehearse conversations, explore fictional characters, or examine decisions. Users should understand that AI-generated responses may be inaccurate, incomplete, overly agreeable, or emotionally persuasive.

Recommended safeguards include clear disclosure that characters are simulated, local-first storage where possible, user control over logs, deletion options, crisis guidance, limits on high-risk use, and reminders to seek real human support when needed.

Kindred’s design emphasizes privacy, transparency, and user agency. The goal is not to replace human relationships or professional care, but to provide a safer, more controlled environment for reflective dialogue.
